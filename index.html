What are cookies?

Much like when Hansel and Gretel marked their trail through the forest by dropping cookie crumbs, cookies on your computer/device help a server or website
store information about your previous visits to that website or a related one.

When are cookies used?

For instance, if you've ordered something from an online retailer before, cookies will have saved your shipping destination that first time and you will automatically
be directed to the page customised for your country.
Cookies can also store what items you put in the cart during your last session or information like your address or credit card information.

How do cookies work?

Cookies are essentially small pieces of data that are sent from a website and stored on your computer/device by your web browser.
By default, cookies are destroyed once you close your browser. However, cookies can be programmed to persist for longer, essentially forever.
There is even such a thing as Zombie Cookies.
